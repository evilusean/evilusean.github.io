How to Use & Unit Measurements

🎯 How to Use

Enter a value in any input field and press Enter to convert to all other angle units. The canvas visualization updates in real-time to show the angle visually.

📐 Units of Measurement

Degrees (°)

The most common unit for measuring angles. A full circle equals 360 degrees. One degree is subdivided into 60 minutes, and one minute into 60 seconds.

Example: A right angle = 90°

Radians (rad)

The standard unit in mathematics and physics. A full circle equals 2π radians (≈ 6.283 radians). Radians measure angles by the length of the arc they subtend on a unit circle.

Example: A right angle = π/2 ≈ 1.5708 radians

Conversion: radians = degrees × (π / 180)

Gradians (grad)

Also called "grads" or "gons," used primarily in surveying and civil engineering. A full circle equals 400 gradians. This system divides a circle into 400 equal parts instead of 360.

Example: A right angle = 100 grad

Conversion: gradians = degrees × (10 / 9)

Degrees/Minutes/Seconds (DD MM SS)

A sexagesimal (base-60) system commonly used in navigation, astronomy, and geography. One degree = 60 minutes (arcminutes), one minute = 60 seconds (arcseconds).

Format: DD MM SS.SS (example: 45 30 25.5 means 45°30'25.5")

Example: A right angle = 90 0 0

Conversion: decimal degrees = DD + (MM/60) + (SS/3600)

💡 Tips

  • Use decimal values for precise conversions (e.g., 45.5 for 45.5°)
  • The canvas shows angles from 0° to 360° (angles normalize to this range)
  • All conversions support floating-point precision
  • For DMS input, use spaces to separate degrees, minutes, and seconds
